abstract: >-
We present SilverCycling, a VR bike-based locomotion technique designed to support spatial orientation for older adults in virtual environments. To examine the impact of natural motion-based locomotion, we conducted a user study with 16 older adults comparing SilverCycling with joystick-based navigation. Results suggest that SilverCycling can improve spatial orientation and enhance user experience in open-road urban environments. Our findings provide insights and design recommendations for developing more accessible and age-friendly VR locomotion systems.
